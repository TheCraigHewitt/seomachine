# Natural Transitions

Use transitions to improve flow and scannability without sounding scripted.

## Core Transition Set

| Use Case | Preferred Phrases |
|----------|-------------------|
| Preview structure | "Here's what we'll cover", "We'll start with X, then Y" |
| Introduce topic | "When it comes to X", "Another key factor is" |
| Move to next section | "Next", "Now let's look at", "With that covered" |
| Refer back | "As mentioned earlier", "Building on that point" |
| Add point | "Also", "Another factor", "On top of that", "Plus" |
| Contrast | "However", "That said", "In contrast", "On the flip side" |
| Cause/effect | "Because of this", "As a result", "This means", "That's why" |
| Example | "For example", "Consider this", "Take X", "Say you want to" |
| Emphasis | "Key takeaway", "What matters most", "This is critical" |
| Evidence | "According to [Source]", "[Source] reports", "Data shows" |
| Recap | "In short", "To recap", "Bottom line" |
| Conclude | "Here's what to do next", "Now it's your turn" |

---

## Question-Based Transitions

Useful for conversational tone and featured snippet optimization:
- "So what does this mean for you?"
- "Why does this matter?"
- "How do you apply this?"
- "Where should you start?"
- "What's the catch?"

---

## Best Practices

1. Match transition formality to audience.
2. Vary phrasing; repeating one connector gets noticed.
3. Use transitions mainly at paragraph starts for scannability.
4. Read aloud and cut anything that sounds forced.
5. Front-load the key word or phrase in the transition.

---

## Phrases to Avoid (AI Tells)

- "That being said"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "At its core"
- "In today's digital landscape"
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of"
- "Let's delve into"
- "In conclusion" (at paragraph start)

See `seo-audit/references/ai-writing-detection.md` for the full anti-pattern list.
