# Launch Strategy

Read `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` if it exists.

## Core Philosophy

Treat launches as a repeated system, not a one-day event.
- Ship in phases.
- Convert attention into owned audience.
- Keep momentum with continuous announcements.

## ORB Framework

### Owned Channels
- Email list, blog, community, product surfaces
- Goal: capture and retain demand you control

### Rented Channels
- Social platforms, marketplaces, third-party discovery
- Goal: acquire reach and route it to owned channels

### Borrowed Channels
- Partners, creators, podcasts, guest placements
- Goal: borrow trust and attention, then convert to owned

### ORB Examples
- **Superhuman**: invite + onboarding model drove exclusivity and referrals.
- **Notion**: social/community distribution funneled users into owned onboarding.
- **TRMNL**: creator review produced large spike; affiliate layer sustained it.

## Five-Phase Launch Approach

### Phase 1: Internal Launch
- Recruit friendly testers
- Validate core workflow and major failure points

### Phase 2: Alpha Launch
- Publish early-access page
- Start controlled external testing

### Phase 3: Beta Launch
- Expand invites in batches
- Start light public storytelling and teaser content

### Phase 4: Early Access Launch
- Leak screenshots/demos
- Collect quantitative usage + qualitative feedback
- Decide throttle strategy (batched vs full early-access invite)

### Phase 5: Full Launch
- Open broad access
- Announce across email, product UI, site, blog, social, launch platforms

## Product Hunt Launch Strategy

### Pros
- Early-adopter distribution
- Credibility and backlink potential

### Risks
- Short-lived spikes
- Ranking competition
- High prep requirement

### Execution Playbook

**Before launch day**
1. Build supporter network.
2. Prepare listing assets (tagline, demo, visuals).
3. Plan all-day response coverage.

**On launch day**
1. Respond to every comment.
2. Keep conversations active.
3. Route traffic to owned signup path.

**After launch day**
1. Follow up with engagers.
2. Convert traffic to email/onboarding flows.
3. Repackage launch assets into ongoing content.

### Case Snapshots
- **SavvyCal**: prep + relationship building + active response -> #2 Product of the Month.
- **Reform**: pre-launch community work + polished assets -> #1 Product of the Day.

## Post-Launch Product Marketing

### Immediate Actions
- Activate onboarding sequence
- Add launch to recurring newsletter
- Update key website pages
- Publish comparison and proof assets
- Offer interactive demo experience

### Keep Momentum
- Re-share launch angles with new evidence
- Publish customer outcomes from first cohort
- Keep lightweight in-app announcements active

## Ongoing Launch Strategy

### Update Priority Matrix

| Update Size | Recommended Motion |
|-------------|--------------------|
| Major | Multi-channel campaign (email, product UI, blog, social) |
| Medium | Targeted segment announcement + in-app placement |
| Minor | Changelog and release notes |

### Operating Rules
- Stagger announcements.
- Reuse what performed previously.
- Keep a steady cadence of visible product progress.

## Launch Checklist

### Pre-Launch
- [ ] Landing page and waitlist live
- [ ] Owned channels prepared
- [ ] Borrowed channel targets listed
- [ ] Launch assets ready
- [ ] Onboarding and tracking ready

### Launch Day
- [ ] Email announcement sent
- [ ] Blog post published
- [ ] Social distribution executed
- [ ] Product surface announcements live
- [ ] Team assigned to monitor/respond

### Post-Launch
- [ ] Follow-up sequence running
- [ ] Feedback reviewed and prioritized
- [ ] Win/loss signals documented
- [ ] Next launch beat planned
