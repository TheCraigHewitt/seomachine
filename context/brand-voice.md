# Bliss Science Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines the Bliss Science brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference this when writing all content to ensure consistency.

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Direct & Unapologetically Real
- **What it means**: No corporate speak, no fluff, no sugar-coating. We say what needs to be said with confidence and clarity, even if it's uncomfortable.
- **How it sounds**: Short, punchy sentences. Strategic use of mild profanity. Honest about challenges. No beating around the bush.
- **Example**: "Ready to light some shit on fire?" or "No cookie-cutter bullshit here; our process together is tailored to your exact situation."
- **Avoid**: Corporate jargon, overly polished language, hedging, apologetic tone, vague promises

### 2. Scientifically Credible Yet Soulfully Human
- **What it means**: We bridge the gap between logic and emotion, science and spirituality. MIT-trained rigor meets somatic wisdom.
- **How it sounds**: Scientific terminology paired with evocative, emotional language. We honor both the rational mind and felt experience.
- **Example**: "Relationship Chemistry™ for men who refuse to settle. Science-backed strategies for deeper connection & unshakeable bonds."
- **Avoid**: Pure woo-woo spiritual talk, dry academic language, choosing one side over the other

### 3. Empowering & Non-Judgmental
- **What it means**: We create a space where successful men can be real about their struggles without shame. We believe in their capacity to transform.
- **How it sounds**: Strength-based language. "You have power." "You can create this." Acknowledgment of challenges without making them wrong.
- **Example**: "This is a space where you can be real about your experiences as a man without being judged, even if what you have to say isn't 'PC.'"
- **Avoid**: Shaming men, therapist-speak that sounds clinical, victim mentality, "men are the problem" narratives

### 4. Results-Driven & Action-Oriented
- **What it means**: We're focused on tangible outcomes, not just insights. Our methods create measurable change.
- **How it sounds**: Active verbs. Specific transformations. Before-and-after clarity. ROI language that speaks to business-minded clients.
- **Example**: "Bliss Science changes you at the root using neuroscience-backed methods—so you don't just know what to do; your system does it automatically under pressure."
- **Avoid**: Vague transformation promises, endless processing without action, theory without practice

### 5. Boldly Provocative & Rebellious
- **What it means**: We challenge conventional wisdom about relationships. We're the relationship rebels who flip the script.
- **How it sounds**: Questions that challenge assumptions. Bold claims backed by results. Invitation to break free from status quo.
- **Example**: "You can keep the status-quo of acrimonious relating and dead-bedrooms, or you can become the relationship rebel, flip the script, and create a legacy of love for your children."
- **Avoid**: Playing it safe, conventional relationship advice, tiptoeing around topics, being milquetoast

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone: Fierce, Warm Authority
Imagine you're a trusted advisor who's "been there" and made it through to the other side. You're MIT-trained so you get how logic-driven men think, but you've also done the deep personal work. You're direct because you care, not because you're harsh. You speak truth even when it's uncomfortable. You're in their corner, believing in their capacity to transform when others might have given up. You're part scientist, part warrior, part guide—and you don't apologize for any of it.

### Tone Variations by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Direct, practical, empowering, step-by-step confidence
- "First, shift how you relate to yourself: No more inner critic running the show"
- "Handle emotions, communication, and boundaries, with skill"
- "Court, 'microflirt,' and connect with her outside the bedroom"

**Strategy/Advice Content**: Bold, insightful, challenging assumptions, blend of science and wisdom
- "The bottom line is... your relationship affects your bottom line"
- "Women are drawn to power, but it's not the kind you think"
- "View this work as an immersive research project"

**Personal Story/About Content**: Vulnerable, authentic, relatable, transformation-focused
- "I had to be dragged kicking and screaming to 'personal growth'"
- "I was that wife who 'didn't want to talk about it'"
- "After I lost everything – house, marriage, career – that I discovered what truly makes a strong bond"

**Service/Program Content**: Results-focused, specific, high-value, exclusive
- "Private mentorship access is limited"
- "I use methods that therapists are neither trained in nor authorized to use"
- "Impeccably discreet and tailored to you"

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure
- **Vary length**: Mix short, punchy sentences (3-5 words) with longer explanatory ones. Use fragments for emphasis.
- **Active voice preferred**: "Transform your love life" not "Your love life can be transformed"
- **Average length**: 10-20 words per sentence, but frequently use 3-7 word sentences for impact
- **Clarity first**: If it requires a second read, simplify it. Left-brained readers want efficiency.

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 1-4 sentences typically; single-sentence paragraphs are powerful
- **One idea per paragraph**: Each paragraph should advance the narrative or make one clear point
- **Transitions**: Use questions, "The truth is," "Here's what happens," to create flow
- **White space**: Generous. Let powerful statements breathe.

### Word Choice
- **Blend High and Low**: Mix sophisticated terminology (non-dual metaphysics, neuroscience) with casual/bold language (shit, bullshit, grok)
- **Tech-Savvy Metaphors**: ROI, rocket fuel vs lead weights, immersive research project, level up
- **Active Power Verbs**: Transform, reboot, rekindle, ignite, light up, unleash, build, create, master
- **Specific Over Generic**: "soul-drenched passion" not "better intimacy"; "King energy" not "confidence"
- **Avoid Fluff**: Cut filler words. Every word should earn its place. Be direct.

### Terminology

**Say This** → **Not That**
- Relationship Chemistry™ → Relationship coaching (our proprietary approach)
- King energy → Alpha male (more evolved, less toxic)
- Get on the same team → Improve communication (more specific outcome)
- Relational IQ → Emotional intelligence (more comprehensive)
- Powerful nerds / left-brained visionaries → Tech guys (more respectful and specific)
- Soul-drenched passion → Great sex (more evocative and holistic)
- Rekindle / ignite / light up → Fix / improve (more dynamic and alive)
- Chemistry call → Discovery call (on-brand terminology)

## Content Formatting

### Headlines
- **Be bold and benefit-specific**: "Transform your love life" not "Relationship Coaching"
- **Include emotional payoff**: Tell them how they'll feel, not just what they'll get
- **Use questions strategically**: "Ready to light some shit on fire?" engages immediately
- **Blend credibility with promise**: "Science-backed strategies for deeper connection"

### Subheadings
- **Action-oriented**: "Build the one bond money can't buy" not "About Our Services"
- **Use imperative voice**: "Bring play, peace, and passion back"
- **Promise transformation**: Every subhead should hint at the change possible
- **Be specific**: "Reconnect with Your Wife" not "Relationship Help"

### Lists
- **Lead with verbs**: "Get inside her head" not "Understanding women"
- **Be concrete**: "No more inner critic running the show" vs "Better self-talk"
- **Keep parallel structure**: Start each item consistently
- **Make them scannable**: Each item should work independently

### Calls-to-Action
- **Use "Chemistry" language**: "Apply for your Chemistry call" not "Book a consultation"
- **Create exclusivity**: "Private mentorship access is limited"
- **Add qualifier**: "*Serious and appropriate inquiries only.*"
- **Action-oriented buttons**: "Get started", "Apply now", "Request private consult"

## Voice Examples

### Excellent Bliss Science Voice ✅

"You've built a world-class business (or three)... Now it's time for a world-class relationship.

The truth is, I had to be dragged kicking and screaming to 'personal growth.' After training at MIT, I loved relying on logic, defending myself, and being right (who doesn't?!).

But both my professional and personal relationships suffered, and my marriage failed. It was only after I lost everything – house, marriage, career – that I discovered what truly makes a strong bond.

Now I help successful, post-exit founders and left-brained visionaries in tech integrate logic and heart without feeling like you want to crawl in a hole and hide. Ready to light some shit on fire?"

**Why this works**:
- Direct address to target audience with specific language ("world-class business")
- Vulnerable personal story that builds credibility and relatability
- Acknowledges the resistance logic-driven people feel toward "soft" work
- Blends high credibility (MIT) with honest struggle (marriage failed)
- Uses strategic profanity to signal authenticity and edge
- Ends with bold, provocative CTA that matches brand personality

### Not Bliss Science Voice ❌

"Welcome to our relationship coaching practice. We offer evidence-based therapeutic interventions designed to help couples enhance their communication patterns and resolve conflict more effectively.

Dr. Jessica Gold utilizes a holistic, client-centered approach that integrates various modalities to support your relational goals. Our sessions provide a safe, non-judgmental space where you can explore your feelings and develop healthier relationship dynamics.

If you're ready to invest in your relationship, we invite you to schedule an initial consultation to discuss your needs and determine if our services are the right fit for you."

**Why this fails**:
- Generic therapist-speak that could be anyone
- Passive, clinical language ("enhance communication patterns")
- No personality, no edge, no differentiation
- Doesn't speak to specific target audience (men in tech)
- Vague promises without specific outcomes
- No vulnerability or personal connection
- Misses the science-meets-soul positioning entirely
- Sounds like every other therapy practice

## Audience Understanding

### Who We Write For

**Primary Audience**: HIGH-ACHIEVING MEN IN TECH & STEM
- Founders (especially post-exit), investors (VC/private equity), engineers
- Logic-driven, analytical, left-brained thinkers who value data and results
- Successful professionally but struggling in romantic relationships
- Skeptical of "therapy speak" and "woo-woo" approaches
- Value discretion and high-level service
- Want specific, actionable strategies—not endless processing
- Post-divorce OR in sexless/disconnected marriages
- Willing to invest in themselves and do the deep work

**Secondary Audiences**:
- **High-achieving women in STEM**: Face similar logic-vs-emotion challenges
- **Couples (both partners engaged)**: Want to work together on transformation
- **Men in other high-pressure fields**: Finance, law, medicine who share similar characteristics
- **Second/third marriages**: Determined not to repeat past patterns

### What They Care About

**Top Priorities**:
1. Results and measurable outcomes (not just "insights")
2. Understanding women and what actually works in relationships
3. Reigniting passion and intimacy (especially in long-term relationships)
4. Maintaining discretion and privacy (many are public figures)
5. Integrating success in professional and personal life

**Pain Points**:
1. Feeling like an imposter—successful everywhere except in love
2. Sexless marriages or living as roommates
3. Being the only partner willing to address relationship issues
4. Not knowing how to access or express emotions effectively
5. Fear of repeating past relationship failures
6. Endless criticism or conflict at home affecting work performance
7. Skepticism about "soft" personal development work

### How to Serve Them

- **Speak their language**: Use tech, business, and scientific metaphors (ROI, rocket fuel, research project)
- **Respect their intelligence**: Don't dumb things down; they can handle complexity
- **Be direct and efficient**: Get to the point. They value their time.
- **Show credibility first**: Lead with MIT PhD, neuroscience background
- **Bridge logic to emotion**: Help them access feelings without shame
- **Promise specific outcomes**: "Re-ignite passion" not "better communication"
- **Acknowledge their skepticism**: "I was dragged kicking and screaming too"
- **Create safe space for vulnerability**: No judgment, even when not "PC"
