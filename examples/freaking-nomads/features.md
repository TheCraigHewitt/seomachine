# Freaking Nomads — Features & Offerings

This document outlines Freaking Nomads' content offerings, products, and community features. Reference this when writing content that mentions FN features, creates CTAs, or compares FN to competitors.

---

## Core Value Proposition

Freaking Nomads is the digital nomad resource built on authentic, first-person experience — not press trips, SEO-farm content, or aspirational marketing. Everything is tested, verified, and written by people who actually live this life.

---

## Content Offerings

### 1. Publication (Articles, Guides & Reviews)

- **What it is**: Editorial content covering every aspect of the digital nomad lifestyle — written by real nomads and contributors with verified firsthand experience
- **Coverage**: 350+ destinations with nomad-specific reviews, visa guides, gear reviews, remote work guides, lifestyle pieces
- **Content types**:
  - Destination guides (pros/cons, costs, internet speeds, coworking, safety)
  - Digital nomad visa guides (eligibility, requirements, process, costs, tax implications)
  - Gear and tech reviews (eSIMs, portable monitors, keyboards, travel insurance, banking)
  - Remote work guides (job boards, home exchange, portable WiFi, time zones)
  - Lifestyle content (health, productivity, community, personal development)
  - Trend and opinion pieces (nomad landscape, policy changes, emerging destinations)
- **Differentiator**: Every article includes real performance data, actual costs, and honest cons — not just highlights

### 2. Newsletter

- **What it is**: Weekly email covering digital nomad lifestyle, resources, and community updates
- **Subscribers**: ~4,000 with strong engagement
- **Format**: Irene Lidia Wang writes the introductory letter each week (personal, conversational, editorial)
- **Content mix**: curated resources, new articles, nomad news, community highlights
- **Differentiator**: Irene's personal voice — not a newsletter that feels like a content dump

### 3. Community / Forum

- **What it is**: Free, open community for digital nomads at all stages
- **Members**: 1,433+
- **Access**: Free to join
- **Value**: Peer-to-peer advice, destination recommendations, visa experiences, job opportunities, connections
- **Differentiator**: Authenticity and inclusivity — not a closed, paid community

### 4. Remote Nomad Jobs (Job Board)

- **What it is**: Curated job board featuring fully remote positions for digital nomads
- **Focus**: Verified remote-only roles (not "hybrid" disguised as remote)
- **Audience**: Nomads seeking employment income while traveling
- **Differentiator**: Nomad-specific curation vs. generic remote job boards

### 5. Events Directory

- **What it is**: Directory of digital nomad events, meetups, retreats, and conferences worldwide
- **Use cases**: Finding community, networking, discovering nomad hubs, planning travel around events
- **Coverage**: Global events, updated regularly

### 6. Destinations Database

- **What it is**: 350+ nomad destination profiles with standardized reviews
- **Data per destination**: Cost of living, internet speeds/reliability, visa situation, coworking options, accommodation types, safety assessment, nomad community size, best time to visit
- **Format**: Consistent structure for easy comparison across destinations

### 7. Workspaces Database

- **What it is**: Curated listings of coworking spaces, coliving spaces, and work-friendly cafés
- **Scope**: Major nomad hubs and emerging destinations
- **Per listing**: Wi-Fi speed, pricing, opening hours, noise level, outlet availability, address

## Audience Segments

### Aspiring Digital Nomads
- Considering the jump but haven't made it yet
- Need: visa info, cost-of-living reality checks, remote job resources, gear recommendations
- FN serves them with: destination guides, visa guides, "how to become a nomad as a [profession]" guides, job boards

### Active Digital Nomads
- Already living nomadically, optimizing the lifestyle
- Need: destination intel, community, gear upgrades, tax/legal info, insurance
- FN serves them with: ongoing destination reviews, gear comparisons, legal/tax guides, community

### Remote Workers (Not Full Nomads)
- Work remotely but may travel periodically or want to
- Need: gear recommendations, destination guides for extended workations, remote job resources
- FN serves them with: gear reviews, café guides, portable WiFi, home exchange guides

### Non-Western Nomads
- Russians, Brazilians, Filipinos, etc. facing different visa constraints
- Need: passport-specific visa info, payment solutions that work globally
- FN serves them with: targeted visa guides, banking reviews, insurance options for their situation

### Senior Nomads
- Older adults (50+) exploring nomadic life
- Need: safety-conscious destinations, health insurance, slower-paced content
- FN serves them with: dedicated content and curated blog recommendations for their demographic

## Competitive Differentiators

### vs. Nomad List (nomadlist.com)
- **FN advantage**: Editorial depth and personal voice — Nomad List provides aggregated scores, FN provides real stories and nuanced assessments
- **FN advantage**: Visa guides with firsthand application experience, not just data points
- **FN advantage**: Community-generated authentic content, not algorithmic rankings

### vs. Travel Blogs (Goats on the Road, etc.)
- **FN advantage**: Remote work-first perspective — every destination assessed for nomads specifically (internet, coworking, cost for remote workers), not tourists
- **FN advantage**: Honest cons and limitations, not aspirational travel content
- **FN advantage**: Real monthly cost breakdowns vs. vague "affordable" claims

### vs. Generic Remote Work Resources
- **FN advantage**: Destination-specific, lived experience — not theoretical remote work advice
- **FN advantage**: Community of real nomads, not corporate HR teams writing about remote work
- **FN advantage**: Coverage of niche topics (visa for specific passports, insurance claims experience, eSIM speeds by city)

## Content Creation Guidelines

When mentioning FN features in articles:

1. **Lead with community value**: Mention community as the network of people who've been there, not as a marketing product
2. **Job board as practical resource**: Remote Nomad Jobs solves a real problem — finding verified fully-remote work
3. **Newsletter as editorial**: Irene's personal letter is the differentiator — not just "a newsletter"
4. **Destinations database for context**: Link to specific destination pages when writing about a location
5. **CTAs at the end only**: Never interrupt article flow with community/newsletter pitches — always reserve for the closing section
